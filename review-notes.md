# Mowatt Marine Redesign Notes

## Current Site Issues

- The logo and location are useful, but the header gives too much height to the logo and pushes the offer down.
- The hero headline is generic and does not immediately say who should hire the company or where it works.
- Primary actions duplicate the same destination, so emergency visitors do not get a clearly different path.
- The service section uses uneven image quality. The strongest assets are the real local water, work barge, damaged dock, and aerial shoreline photos.
- The generated dock render and network infographic reduce trust because they look less like real field work.
- The reviews section is empty, which makes the site feel unfinished.
- The contact section is useful but visually late and does not prioritize phone or WhatsApp for urgent work.

## Redesign Direction

- Visual direction: Pacific working waterfront, with marine ink, cedar, signal orange, real local imagery, and chart-like linework.
- Structure: lead with offer, service area, emergency path, and service categories before explaining the network model.
- Content: use concrete, plain-language service descriptions and remove empty testimonial framing.
- Conversion: persistent phone/WhatsApp paths, a clearer project intake form, and emergency-specific calls to action.
- Assets kept: logo, Indian Arm hero, work barge, damaged dock, and aerial local waters imagery.
- Assets avoided: generated dock construction image and low-legibility network infographic.
